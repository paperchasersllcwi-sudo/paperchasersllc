Help With Difficult Vehicle-Title Situations

Vehicle-title problems are rarely solved by one form alone. Different procedures may apply depending on who last owned the vehicle, how it was obtained, whether a title record exists, whether a lien appears, and whether the vehicle was abandoned, purchased without a title, inherited, stored, repaired, or transferred through another process.

Paper Chasers LLC provides administrative assistance with collecting the available information, identifying missing documents, completing applicant-provided portions of forms, preparing supporting correspondence, and organizing a submission packet for review.

Each assignment begins with a review of the facts and records. We then define the administrative work we can perform and explain what additional information or outside assistance may be needed.

Our objective: Turn a confusing collection of vehicle records, receipts, communications, photographs, and forms into an organized title-assistance file with a clearly defined next step.

Private Vehicle Owners

Assistance for people who purchased or received a vehicle but did not receive a usable certificate of title.

Property and Business Owners

Administrative support involving vehicles left at properties, repair facilities, storage locations, businesses, or other premises.

Wisconsin-Based Service

Based in Appleton and serving the Fox Valley, surrounding communities, and title-assistance clients throughout Wisconsin.

Important: The phrase “abandoned vehicle” has a specific legal and administrative meaning. A vehicle being left on private property does not automatically give the property owner legal ownership or the right to obtain a title.

Bonded Title Administrative Support

A bonded title may be considered in certain cases where a person has possession of a vehicle and evidence of ownership but cannot provide the ordinary title documents required for a standard transfer.

The reviewing agency determines whether the bonded-title process is available, what evidence is sufficient, the required bond amount, and whether additional documents or another procedure are necessary.

Paper Chasers LLC can help organize the initial application, applicant-provided ownership explanation, purchase records, vehicle information, photographs, correspondence, and requested supporting materials.

We Do Not Issue Bonds

A required surety bond must be obtained from an authorized bonding or insurance provider. Paper Chasers LLC is not a surety company or insurance agency.

Agency Approval Is Required

Preparing an application does not establish eligibility and does not guarantee that a bond request or title will be approved.

Ownership Disputes Are Different

Competing ownership claims, fraud allegations, lien disputes, estate disputes, or demands for a court order may require legal advice or court action.

Never purchase a surety bond merely because someone informally says one is needed. The applicant should first receive the applicable instructions or bond requirement from the reviewing authority.

Information to Gather Before Contacting Us

  • Vehicle identification number, if readable;
  • Vehicle year, make, model, and body style;
  • Current vehicle location;
  • Photographs of the vehicle and VIN locations;
  • Any title, registration, or ownership document;
  • Bill of sale or purchase agreement;
  • Receipts or payment records;
  • Seller’s name and last known contact information;
  • Copies of messages or communications with the seller;
  • Information about any known prior owner;
  • Available lienholder or lender information;
  • Towing, repair, or storage records;
  • Police, municipal, or county documentation;
  • Notices previously mailed or received;
  • WisDOT letters, forms, or rejection notices;
  • Information explaining how possession was obtained;
  • Known title brands or salvage history; and
  • Any court, probate, estate, or repossession documents.
Do not alter, remove, replace, restamp, conceal, or damage a VIN plate or identifying number. Do not create, backdate, or modify a bill of sale or ownership document. All information submitted must be truthful.

Service Limitations

No Ownership Determination

Paper Chasers LLC does not determine who legally owns a vehicle or decide whether one person’s ownership claim is superior to another person’s claim.

No Legal Advice

We do not interpret vehicle-title laws for clients, recommend litigation, advise clients to assert liens, or select legal remedies.

No Government Authority

We do not issue titles, registrations, transfer certificates, court orders, ownership findings, VIN inspections, or agency approvals.

No Guaranteed Result

Administrative preparation does not guarantee acceptance of an application or issuance of a Wisconsin certificate of title.

No Improper Vehicle Disposal

We do not authorize towing, sale, scrapping, dismantling, or disposal of a vehicle and do not advise clients to take possession unlawfully.

No Dealer or Towing Representation

Paper Chasers LLC does not represent itself as a licensed motor vehicle dealer, salvage dealer, towing service, surety provider, or government title agent.

A licensed Wisconsin attorney should be consulted when a matter involves disputed ownership, contested liens, estate ownership, fraud, conversion, repossession, court orders, or other legal claims.
