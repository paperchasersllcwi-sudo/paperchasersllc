Administrative Support for Your Pardon Application

A pardon application may require substantial personal history, conviction information, rehabilitation records, employment information, references, and a thoughtful written explanation of why relief is being requested.

Gathering that information and presenting it in an organized manner can be difficult. Paper Chasers LLC provides practical administrative support throughout the preparation process.

Our work focuses on organization, document preparation, communication, completeness, presentation, and documented follow-through.

Our objective: Help you prepare a complete, organized, professional application packet that accurately reflects your history, rehabilitation, accomplishments, and reasons for requesting a pardon.

Wisconsin-Focused Assistance

Our service is designed for individuals preparing pardon materials relating to Wisconsin state convictions.

Individualized Preparation

Each application is developed from the applicant’s own history, records, circumstances, achievements, and goals.

Statewide Availability

Assistance is available to applicants throughout Wisconsin, including Appleton, Green Bay, Oshkosh, the Fox Valley, and surrounding communities.

Important service limitation: Paper Chasers LLC is not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice, determine legal eligibility, offer legal opinions, represent applicants before the governor or any board, or guarantee that an application will be accepted, heard, or granted.

What We Can Help With

Application Completion Support

Administrative assistance entering applicant-provided information into the applicable pardon forms and identifying unanswered or incomplete sections.

Personal Statement Preparation

Help organizing, drafting, proofreading, and professionally formatting a personal statement based on your own facts, experiences, and reasons for seeking relief.

Personal History Organization

Assistance organizing employment, education, treatment, volunteer work, family responsibilities, community involvement, accomplishments, and post-conviction progress.

Conviction Information Organization

Help organizing applicant-provided case numbers, conviction information, sentencing information, discharge information, and related records.

Supporting Document Assembly

Assistance organizing certificates, records, awards, employment materials, educational achievements, treatment records, and other relevant supporting documents.

Reference and Support Letters

Administrative assistance identifying potential letter writers, providing general submission instructions, and organizing completed support letters.

Packet Review

A final administrative review for organization, readability, missing responses, obvious inconsistencies, labeling, and document order.

Submission Preparation

Assistance preparing the completed materials for submission according to the instructions applicable at that time.

All facts, dates, explanations, and supporting materials must be provided or approved by the applicant. Paper Chasers LLC does not invent facts, alter records, obtain false statements, conceal convictions, or submit information known to be inaccurate.

Our Preparation Process

1

Initial Intake

We gather preliminary information about your convictions, sentence completion, personal history, and reasons for requesting assistance.

2

Scope Confirmation

We explain the administrative services requested, expected materials, pricing, responsibilities, and anticipated preparation process.

3

Document Collection

You provide the application information, records, certificates, letters, case information, and other materials needed for the agreed scope.

4

Draft Preparation

We organize the materials and prepare agreed application sections, statements, summaries, or supporting documents for your review.

5

Applicant Review

You review the materials, verify the facts, identify corrections, and approve the final wording and supporting documents.

6

Final Organization

We perform the agreed administrative review and organize the completed materials for submission.

What Applicants Should Be Prepared to Provide

  • Full legal name and current contact information;
  • Previous names or aliases, when applicable;
  • Wisconsin court case numbers and counties;
  • Conviction and sentencing information;
  • Sentence-completion or discharge information;
  • Employment and education history;
  • Residence history when requested by the application;
  • Community service and volunteer activities;
  • Treatment, counseling, or rehabilitation records;
  • Certificates, licenses, training, awards, or achievements;
  • Information about family and community responsibilities;
  • Potential references or support-letter writers;
  • A truthful account of the offense and its consequences;
  • Evidence of accountability and positive change;
  • The specific reasons a pardon is being requested; and
  • Other materials required by the current application.
Do not send original irreplaceable documents unless specifically requested. Keep copies of all records and completed materials for your own files.

Important Limitations

No Eligibility Determination

We may help compare applicant-provided information with published application instructions, but we do not make a legal determination that an applicant qualifies for a pardon.

No Legal Representation

We do not act as your attorney, communicate as legal counsel, appear on your behalf, or represent you in hearings or proceedings.

No Guaranteed Outcome

A professionally organized packet cannot guarantee consideration, an interview, a hearing, favorable review, or a pardon.

No Expungement Determination

Questions about expungement eligibility, reopening a criminal judgment, post-conviction relief, sentence modification, or other legal remedies should be directed to a licensed Wisconsin attorney.

Government forms, procedures, eligibility standards, instructions, and submission requirements may change. Current official instructions control over information appearing on this website or in earlier materials.
