Professional Support for Document-Heavy Work

Legal matters, business projects, and administrative assignments often involve large amounts of information, multiple deadlines, and numerous documents that must be collected, organized, tracked, delivered, or prepared for review.

Paper Chasers LLC provides project-based administrative and litigation support tailored to the specific assignment. We help clients organize materials, coordinate tasks, maintain records, prepare documents from client-provided information, and complete practical support work within an agreed scope.

Services may be provided for a one-time assignment, an active case, a document project, or continuing administrative needs, depending on availability and the nature of the work.

Our approach: Confirm the assignment, define the scope, identify the deadline, organize the information, document the work performed, and communicate the next step.

For Attorneys and Law Firms

Overflow administrative support, document organization, case-file preparation, courier coordination, service support, and attorney-directed research assistance.

For Individuals and Businesses

Practical help organizing records, completing administrative paperwork, preparing correspondence, tracking projects, and managing document-related tasks.

Administrative Support Services

Document Organization

Sorting, labeling, indexing, grouping, and organizing records, correspondence, exhibits, receipts, photographs, reports, and other project materials.

Document Preparation

Typing, formatting, proofreading, and assembling documents from information, instructions, templates, or drafts supplied by the client or supervising attorney.

Correspondence Support

Preparing professional letters, notices, summaries, emails, and administrative communications based on client-approved content and instructions.

Records and File Management

Creating organized digital or physical project files, naming documents consistently, maintaining document lists, and helping clients identify missing materials.

Forms and Application Support

Administrative assistance entering client-provided information into forms and applications, reviewing for unanswered fields, and preparing materials for client review and signature.

Timeline and Event Summaries

Organizing client-provided records into chronological timelines, event summaries, communication logs, or issue-based reference materials.

Project Tracking

Maintaining task lists, status records, deadlines, document requests, contact logs, and follow-up items for approved assignments.

Administrative Intake

Collecting preliminary information, organizing client materials, identifying missing records, and preparing information for review by the client, business, or attorney.

Litigation Support Services

Case-File Organization

Organizing pleadings, correspondence, discovery materials, exhibits, photographs, service records, and other documents into a usable case-file structure.

Exhibit Preparation

Labeling, numbering, indexing, arranging, and assembling client-approved or attorney-approved exhibits for review, delivery, filing, or hearing preparation.

Discovery Material Organization

Sorting, naming, indexing, and organizing discovery documents, communications, photographs, transaction records, and related materials under client or attorney direction.

Service and Delivery Coordination

Coordinating process service, legal courier assignments, document delivery, courthouse runs, and delivery confirmation.

Filing Support

Administrative assistance preparing approved documents for filing, organizing attachments, creating filing copies, and coordinating physical or electronic delivery when permitted.

Hearing and Meeting Materials

Organizing document packets, reference binders, timelines, contact sheets, exhibit lists, and client-approved materials for meetings, hearings, or case review.

Attorney-Directed Research Support

Administrative legal research support may be provided to attorneys or law firms under their direction, including locating publicly available authorities, records, and source materials.

Case Activity Summaries

Preparing factual summaries of client-provided records, court activity, communications, transactions, or document histories without offering legal conclusions.

Filing support means administrative preparation and delivery of client-approved or attorney-approved materials. Paper Chasers LLC does not determine which pleading, motion, claim, defense, or legal remedy should be filed.

How the Process Works

1

Submit the Request

Tell us what needs to be completed, the location, the deadline, the materials involved, and the desired result.

2

Define the Scope

We identify the administrative tasks requested, responsibilities, timing, deliverables, pricing, and any information still needed.

3

Provide Materials

The client or supervising attorney provides the documents, instructions, templates, facts, approvals, and access needed for the assignment.

4

Work Is Completed

Paper Chasers LLC performs the agreed administrative, litigation support, courier, or document-related tasks.

5

Review and Corrections

Draft materials are returned for factual verification, approval, corrections, or further instruction when applicable.

6

Delivery and Closeout

Final materials, delivery confirmation, status information, or completed work are provided according to the agreed scope.

Service Boundaries

No Legal Advice

We do not interpret the law for clients, recommend legal action, evaluate legal rights, or advise clients about what they should do in a legal matter.

No Legal Representation

We do not act as an attorney, appear in court as legal counsel, negotiate legal disputes, or communicate on a client’s behalf as a legal representative.

No Independent Legal Drafting

We do not independently select or create legal claims, defenses, pleadings, motions, or legal strategies for members of the public.

No Guaranteed Result

Administrative support, document preparation, filing coordination, research, or delivery services do not guarantee a legal, financial, business, or procedural outcome.

Work involving legal documents must be based on client-provided information, approved templates, official forms, or instructions from a licensed attorney. Clients remain responsible for reviewing, approving, signing, filing, and using all completed materials.
